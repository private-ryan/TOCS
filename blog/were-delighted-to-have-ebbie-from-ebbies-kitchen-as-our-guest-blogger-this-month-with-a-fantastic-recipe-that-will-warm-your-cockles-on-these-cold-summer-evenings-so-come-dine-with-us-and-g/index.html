We’re delighted to have Ebbie from Ebbies Kitchen as our Guest Blogger this month. With a fantastic recipe that will warm your cockles on these cold summer evenings. So come dine with us and give it a go!

25 Jun 2012 / No Comments

Roasted Sweet potato, butternut squash, caramelized onions and curly kale cakes, served with watercress and roasted red pepper vinaigrette and crumbled feta.

Serves 4 people

For the cakes: 4 large sweet potatoes 3 large butternut squash 6 large red onions 2tbsp Balsamic vinegar 1tbsp brown sugar 4 slices of bread to make breadcrumbs ½ tsp cumin Hand full chopped fresh parsley 200 grams washed curly kale 1 lemon

Red pepper and red wine vinaigrette:

  •  ½ cup red wine vinegar
  • 1 ½ cups olive oil
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1tsp Dijon mustard
  • ½ clove crushed garlic
  • 2 roasted diced red peppers
  • Sprinkle thyme

Salad:

  •  Washed watercress
  • Washed grapes
  • Feta cheese

Recipe:

Wash the sweet potatoes, and butternut squash and slice in half (make sure you remove all seeds from the squash). Sprinkle the cumin over the top, salt and pepper and add a drizzle of olive oil. Bake in oven for 30- 45 minutes at 200 degrees/Gas mark 6 or until soft. Slice peppers into strips and place onto an oven tray; drizzle with olive oil and add salt, pepper and thyme. Bake for 20-25minutes.

Meanwhile put the water onto boil for the kale. Slice the red onions into half moon shapes fry for 10 minutes with the garlic. Then add the sugar and balsamic-fry for a further 15- 20 minutes until soft, dark and sweet.

Add the kale to the boiling water and cook for 5 minutes. Place the bread in the magi mix and blend till they become breadcrumbs.

Once all your ingredients are prepared add them all together except the red peppers. Add a squeeze of lemon juice salt and pepper. Form into cakes and shallow fry in a pan.

To make the dressing: Add all ingredients together except the feta cheese.

Place cakes onto a warm plate, add the watercress, sliced grapes and crumble the feta over the cakes and drizzle the vinaigrette on top.

You could serve this dish with sea bass, gammon steak, or pan-fried mixed mushrooms.

Yummy!!
