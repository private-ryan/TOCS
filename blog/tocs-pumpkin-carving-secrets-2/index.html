TOCS Pumpkin Carving Secrets

By rebecca / 09 Oct 2012 / No Comments

How do you carve the perfect pumpkin? Well, we’ve put together a few pointers from years gone by.

Preparation:

Step one: Start by washing and drying your pumpkin to remove any dirt and look for the perfect side of the pumpkin to carve.

Step two: Design an image or sketch to go onto your pumpkin. We suggest using tracing paper – as that way you can transfer the design onto the pumpkin surface using a ball point pen. This way you can see clearly what it is you’re cutting out. Just make sure if you’re using text that the letters are not back-to-front on application.

Step three: You don’t need to buy special tools for carving, but be sure to have good quality tools on hand.Â  Useful ones are a large ice cream scoop, large knife and a smaller sharp knife for detail. Be sure to use the tools on a flat, steady surface and never cut towards yourself. You won’t get extra marks for real blood and guts in this competition!

Let the carving begin: Please take necessary safety precautions

Step one: First, cut a large hole in the pumpkin creating a lid, this can be located on the top or bottom – depending on your design. After you’ve cut out the lid, scoop out the flesh and seeds with your ice cream scoop and put this aside (you never know it might be useful for your design!). The more flesh you remove, the more light will shine through the skin of the fruit, making your design easier to see.

Step two: When carving, cut on the outside of your lines, that way any mistakes can be rectified with minimal damage to the design. It is best to use a small sharp knife to get accurate detail and prevent any accidents. Cut in layers or small pieces so you don’t hurt yourself or take a chunk out of that pumpkin! When you’re happy with your what you’ve done, rub cooking oil on any exposed edges to prevent the fruit from browning. And… Voila! A finished pumpkin, fit for a competition. Just pop a tea-light into the pumpkin and you can see your art work in all its glory! (For safety reasons we must advise that you never leave a flame unattended).
