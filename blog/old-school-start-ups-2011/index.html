Old School Start ups 2011- thank you to all!

By rebecca / 25 Jun 2012 / No Comments

Well Old School Start ups is at an end, and it’s been a really great week. Monday’s launch opened its doors to two hundred people, who visited the Pop up Gallery featuring as part of the business festival, and the ten minute clinics were a sell out.  Thanks to Gavin Eddy, Ed Thomas, David Evans,Nicola Whyte, Jennie Wood, Hayley Whitten, Allison Herbert, Adam Harling, Debbie Boulton and Dave Smith for being such great sports.   Local businesses and residents of Frome enjoyed Milk St Brewery’s Funky Monkey and Yapp Brothers red and white Saumur.   Yapp Brothers did a great wine tasting session,  Allison Collard from 1.2 (china) Ltd based in the hub tells us a bit about the tasting event “We were treated to 5 wines and Champagne, which was my personal favorite – Champagne Yapp Brut NV was a light champagne but not lacking in taste, very classy!   I was also surprised by the elegance of the sweet wine, until now I have never liked any sweet wine, but this one (Jurancon Moelleux: Domaine Bellegarde 2008) had a sweet and acidic mix which was very very drinkable, not to mention the Rose from Corsica.  I would highly recommend a visit to the Yapp Brothers Vineyard.”

A big thank you to everyone who ran really inspiring workshops; Ed Thomas, David Evans, Luke Manning and Harry Neubert, Gavin Eddy, Andy Britnell and Judy Heminsley. We would like to say a huge thanks to everyone who has participated in the events throughout the festival and a big thanks to our generous sponsors: Pencil, Fancy, Frome.tv, Ebbies Kitchen (just the most amazing mix of flavours we’ve ever tasted, and the brownies she made, well once you have a taste,your senses take over and you simply can’t concentrate on anything else!), Yapp Brothers, Milk St Brewery, and The Cornerhouse. A special thanks to Philip Lambert, who was our mystery guest speaker, and provided a very intimate night to end on. This event would not have been possible without the generosity of everyone involved and we feel very fortunate to be based in such a great town as Frome. Here’s to Frome and all the businesses who kindly gave their time to make Old School Start-ups happen!
