How a business plan can help you understand your desire to run a business

By rebecca / 20 Feb 2013 / No Comments

Do you have the dream of becoming your own boss and working the hours that suit your time and your family? Does taking the first steps to making that happen make your blood run cold, because you just don’t know how to turn your ideas into a steadfast plan? Why not just jump right in and create your business plan, make believe you’re pitching the idea to someone else – not just yourself.  Soon the plan will come together making you realise just how much you need to start-up your own enterprise.

Don’t view your business plan as just a formal document!  It can be an exciting way of translating your mission; vision; values and goals to everyone you need to help you turn your new business idea into a reality. Here is a great archive of sample plans for almost every business model to get you started!

Sitting down and mapping out your plan means you’ll have already undertaken a vital process when starting up…Research! You will need to have an idea and  understanding about any challenges and opportunities you face in your chosen market or industry. This will give you an idea about whether your dream to run your business is from the point of becoming a business owner, or if you are dreaming about the perfect cafe or gym from the angle of the customer!  The two worlds are very different. Consider and draft strategies and tactics that will accommodate and respond to the needs of your future clients. Will you meet them? Do you want to meet them anymore? Does this venture still sound as attractive as it did before you started the planning process? This in-depth view of your start-up allows you to examine your idea’s viability and success.

Any business model has competition and your success will be determined by how you set out your business from the start! Check out other competitors and try to ascertain what they have or have not done right. What would you have done differently with marketing campaigns or customer service? Putting this all in your business plan enables you to have insight into your industry before you’ve even started trading. With this valuable upper-hand you can decide how to set yourself above established competitors, as well as know where to place your products, services and value.

Your business plan will allow you to prepare for any ‘what-if’ situations. Meaning that you automatically create multiple back-up plans should your master plan turn out differently than you expected, a situation which happens more often than you’d think – to many entrepreneurs and business owners. This is a thought to ponder over – will this reality of things possibly not working out change your desire to go ahead with starting up?

Thinking way in to the future; having a well thought out business plan will pave out the reality of the big picture. Including a time-line for when to put certain parts of your plan in to practice, this helps to map out a clear vision about the operations of your business. Meaning you can already cover where you want to be in the early stages of growth and what you need to action to ensure you’re on track with progress.

Still going ahead with your new business? Great. The better you appreciate your idea the more effective you’ll be in creating the right tone for your business; management team and employees. Don’t be one of the many businesses out there with good products and market potential, that either didn’t make it or didn’t fulfill their ambitions because no-one had the appropriate experience, skills or talent to take the company where it needed to go, in order to go the distance.

Starting up a new business can be time consuming, having a business plan can help you and your loved ones understand the volume of time, money and  other commitments you’ll need to dedicate to the project.  Why not include their input in your business plan?  This way they will feel included and will have a more empathic committed approach to what you want to achieve – with a common set of goals. This is also a way to include issues and how to reconcile any differences you all may have onto a unified contract.

Enjoy this initial process, your idea may evolve even in this first step to fulfilling your desire to run your own enterprise. Realise it’s not the right time? At least you know, and you’ve explored the idea. That is a big step in itself! Don’t feel constrained by the idea of a plan, anything can change and having a basic outline about what you might do in any situation can mean that your business will flourish when others don’t.
