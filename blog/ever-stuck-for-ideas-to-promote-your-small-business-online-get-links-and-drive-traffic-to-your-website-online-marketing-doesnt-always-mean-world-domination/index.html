Ever stuck for ideas to promote your small business online? Get links, and drive traffic to your website? Online marketing doesn’t always mean world domination…

25 Jun 2012 / No Comments

You don’t have to be a marketing maverick- Why not love and live local, and the rest of the world is sure to follow!Here are a few ideas…

1: Start at the very beginning: If you have a website or are thinking of taking that step, make sure it’s straight-forward and simple.  Try resisting the urge to jazz it up with fancy pants graphics that don’t say or do much.  Offer visitors useful content, links to interesting stuff, even other blogs and other sites, especially stuff going on in your local area.

2: What’s your Goss: Up to something? If you are-shout about it! A blog for your business gives you the opportunity to share your news, knowledge and expertise with a local and larger audience. Let people know that you’re interesting and worth checking out! Add frequent updates to keep people wanting more and include local news! Local doesn’t mean humdrum, if it’s of interest to you-it’ll be of interest to someone else…Aurora St Clare had news that caused quite a stir!

3: Advertising: If you do fancy placing an online ad for your business, you can pop a local one on <a href=”http://www.gumtree.com/”>gumtree</a>-more often than not, people use the internet to find businesses and services in their local area.

4: There’s no business like show business: Why not make an online film about what it is you do or can offer. By doing this, you can gain credibility and allow your clients and any audience members get to know you, and what you’re all about. People share what they like and if they like your presentation, then you’re bound to get noticed!

5: <b>Go yocal: Yahoo local and Google local are directories structured by category and location. You can get a basic listing in Yahoo Local  for free! People looking for your industry or business can find your listings and thus find you!

6: Get rated: Part of Yahoo Local listings are reviews. Get clients to give you a shining review. Businesses with good ratings will climb up the listing ranks.

7: Dot to dot: Join LinkedIn and get connected with people you know. You can also connect with people you don’t through introductions. Join in with conversations and give advice with your know-how. After all, it’s nice to be nice! LinkedIn is also a great way to get recommendations from people you have worked with and think you’re brilliant!

8: Get to know other peeps: Whatever it is you do, your customers will use some kind of social media platform. Have a chat and get to know them more-in turn they’ll share what it is you do and what you’ve been up to!

9: Sign on the dotted line: It’s a good idea to add an email signature-this can reflect your personality, provide important info, and by adding a link to your website, you can draw attention to your blog and what else you can offer! Why not add a catchy phrase! Be sure to change it now and then-so people notice it again.

10: Two heads are better than one: Think about other people in your local business community and consider what it is you offer-can you partner up and share your products, create offers and generate more local interest?
