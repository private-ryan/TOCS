Ever wondered what it takes to manage a work hub? There’s more than meets the eye…

By rebecca / 25 Jun 2012 / 1 Comment

Ten Tips for Managing a Work Hub

1.Understand Your Client

The two main reasons people choose to work in a coworking space is to meet other interesting people/businesses and it’s also cost effective office space, as “pay as you go” tariffs can be financially beneficial for freelancers and small businesses. Ensure your space is designed so that people can mingle well. Make sure you introduce new members and make them feel welcome and part of the community from the word go.

2.Be Choosy With Members

A coworking space is not for everyone. Sometimes it can be hard to adjust from working at home or in a quiet office where you are only used to hearing your own voice and thoughts. A work hub can be a noisy place and you have to learn to zone out background noise. However it is as much about the community, as the working space itself and the hustle and bustle of a buzzing work space can be a welcomed environment to work in. Because of this, it’s wise to make sure that anyone joining the space is aware and committed to the concept of coworking and will mix well with existing members.

3.Embrace a Wide Group of People

Embracing a wide range of clients from different backgrounds and industries can enrich your coworking community and lead to more collaboration work opportunities, benefitting not only your clients but you as well!

4.Monitor Use

Monitoring use is essential for improvements to the building as well as how members use space.  This can be beneficial for extending or opening a new hub in the future, as well as making sure people are comfortable in their working environment.

5.Encourage Members to Jump Ship

More and more coworking spaces are popping up around the country and across the world.  Introducing other local or international coworking spaces to your members might help them revive their work routine and aid in making new connections with other businesses that could both benefit their work and your coworking space!

6.Personal Connection

Encourage members to connect with the space; you want to make them feel at home. You may find that most members will favour a particular desk, let them know they can put up family pictures, postcards or even artwork to put their individual stamp on their space. Anything you can do to personalise your service can have a positive effect, personal mail boxes and hand delivering important packages can make someone feel they matter, which of course they do!

7.Have Your Cake and Eat It

Putting on regular events in a coworking space not only creates a friendly atmosphere, it helps members mix and connect in a social context as well as business. Having events that are simple, but imaginative is key to getting people to mingle. “Paper Aeroplane Competitions” or “Cake Wednesday” can bring people together and such events can take on a life of their own. Try to encourage people to join in and come up with their own suggestions.

8.Be Flexible

Working with people means being human, mistakes can be made and people can double book themselves. Do your best to ensure that you can compromise and accommodate these errors when they occur. Having an additional space like a lounge or resource room that doubles up as a meeting space can be valuable to your members and can be a welcomed place to go if an important phone call needs to be made.

9.Keep In the Loop

Promoting what’s going on in the hub and the business community can be of interest to non-members as well as clients. Sending out a monthly newsletter which features client news can be a great way of giving something back to your members.

10.Make Sure There’s Such Thing As a Free Lunch

Giving something back to the local and business community can make someone’s day! Hosting a free film night, talk or workshop can give local artists or small businesses exposure. In this hard economic climate every penny matters, by giving someone free information or guidance you can really make a difference. Events such as workshops, not only gets people through the door, but it’s also great image management for your coworking space. However, make sure you are genuinely interested and passionate about what it is you are hosting.

So that’s it, little things we try to do here at TOCS to make clients feel it’s somewhere they’d like to be!
