What’s occurring? How to create a newsletter that will keep people interested in you and your business

By rebecca / 10 Sep 2012 / No Comments

As a small business it’s important to keep in touch with people.  Newsletters can be a great marketing tool as they can be used to highlight your products or services.  How exactly do you go about creating a newsletter that will keep people interested in you and your business? And how do you get people to subscribe to your news? We’ve put together a few tips we hope you find useful.

  1. A newsletter isn’t always just about you! Keep in mind what others will be interested in. Anything that grabs your attention is bound to excite others and sharing news you find interesting adds a personal touch – letting people know what you are really all about.
  2. Find out what works and what doesn’t: Why not ask people what they like about your newsletter – or if you’ve not started one yet, ask your customers what news they’d like to receive. You might be surprised by the outcome and involving clients in this way can make them feel more involved with your business and it’s also a great marketing tool to engage with people.
  3. Be realistic about your newsletter: Will it be weekly, monthly or by-monthly, or even less frequent than that? It is about quality not quantity! Make it worth someone opening up your email/letter and giving their time to read your news.
  4. What do you like out there? If you feel that writing a newsletter is a bit intimidating then use prompts to get going. Why not read articles from magazines/newspapers you like and get the tone they use and tailor it to your news? Are you looking to send out a formal newsletter, or do you want it to be a more informal and friendly way to contact people? Get to know what you like and what style/tone would suit your business, write as you speak and have confidence in your own abilities!
  5. Give your letter some life:  Give your newsletter a name. Here at TOCS we have ‘In the Loop’.  This keeps people up to date with what we’re up to and what other local businesses are doing.  Make sure you include photos in your newsletter and have a balance of humour, human interest and your business news. Make sure you write headlines that reflect your content matter.  An ideal newsletter is one that is a joy to read!
  6. Think ahead: If you can, create an editorial calender 3 or 4 months ahead. If you can’t commit to a time frame that far, then at least think a month ahead at a time. It’s also great to time news or events with something relevant – for example mention a start-up story or your own story  of enterprise when it’s Global Entrepreneurship Week (12-18 November 2012).
  7. Dress your news up: Use software that reflects your business or that you feel comfortable using. There are many, many options out there like MailChimp,Comm100, SendEmails to name but a few. Whatever floats your boat, is best for you! We use Createandsend.com and have the fabulous Ed Thomas at Doublshot design our layout
  8. Add a few bits and pieces: Consider including a feature or column within your newsletter, something that is a regular spot. We have “What’s Hot and What’s Not” which highlights quirky and interesting news stories going on in the big wide world. Include articles that illustrate your accomplishments and success stories, as this will tell the audience about what you’re capable of as a business.
  9. Don’t let your words confuse people:Above all make your newsletter accessible to all. You don’t want to alienate people or potential new customers by using jargon only you know.
  10. Who else does what you do? Don’t forget to monitor news stories from your industry, as whatever your main feature story is for your newsletter, chances someone else may be thinking or doing something similar. Why not use your news to get attention from others in your industry as well as your clients. Why not archive your news? This might entice people to subscribe and make sure that you keep on top of old content.

We hope that you find these tips useful and enjoy writing your news!
