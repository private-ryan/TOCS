Branding – and the right way to go about it!

By rebecca / 25 Jun 2012 / No Comments

Branding

Luke Manning Creative Director Pencil Studio Ltd is our guest blogger, giving us wise words on how to create the prefect brand for your small business!

A brand is much more than a logo or identity: it’s more complex than a set of colours or choice of font. True, all of these play a part in building a brand with consistency but what really makes a “brand”? Every day we are bombarded with adverts, packaging and literature, but what companies stick in our minds-which ones do we buy into? A lot of what we decide to purchase in life, be it a car or a soft drink is down to our choice of lifestyle. A good example of this is Innocent Smoothies, we all love the naive approach to the design and you warm to the character and tone of voice of the packaging. You feel that you are buying a quality product from a small company. It’s easy to forget that they are owned by Coca Cola! So why is branding important, especially to smaller businesses? The answer is we all need to have a face to what we do, to express who we are and the kind of businesses we run.  This is achieved by ensuring you brand yourselves correctly, it doesn’t mean packaging yourself in a manner which is not true or does not fully represent your business. It means playing on your strengths! If your clients like the fact that you are easy to work with, friendly and approachable; then this is a key element to build your brand. Use a friendly tone of voice in your writing, use approachable colours and images that are real and not from a loyalty free photo library because they look professional, as these just look fake. If your business is respected for being a straight cut, professional outfit who handles their clients with a no fuss business approach; then let this be the foundations in which your brand is developed. To sum it up briefly, branding is about developing a personality for your company.  Like people they all have them, it’s just finding out what yours is, then nurturing it and using it to your advantage.
