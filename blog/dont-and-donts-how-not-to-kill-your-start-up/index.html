Don’t and don’ts – How not to kill your start up!

By rebecca / 20 Jul 2012 / No Comments

Starting up is a scary business! There are lots of things to consider and not everything goes to plan, or even how you’d imagined it in the first place! So how do you succeed in business? What can you do, to make sure you give yourself the best chance to get your idea off the ground?

1. Don’t leave yourself high and dry: This is most important; you need to make sure your new enterprise can support you before you hand in the notice on that 9-5 job. If you don’t think it can, then work part-time, you can still be employed on a part time basis and start-up!

2. Don’t think you need to have everything shiny and new: You can claim a certain percentage back on equipment that you will use for your business. You can include these items in your pre-trading expenses (it is best to see a bookkeeper or accountant for advice on this!). If you can start-up with what you’ve got, there is no need to waste money on things you don’t really need.

3. Don’t jump off the cliff without a safety net: Just because you will be running the show, with your own hours, without that somebody to answer to – doesn’t mean you don’t need a business plan. Have an idea of how you will develop your business, get new clients, and know what your survival budget is going to be.  A good business is built on a foundation of ideas and with direction in mind.

4. Tick tock on the clock: Time management can be hard to get the hold of if you’re used to working for someone else. Now it’s all down to you!  It is wise to give everyday an agenda and stick to it. If you decide to work from home, you’ll need to set in boundaries – when is work time and when is family time. After all – you’ll still have all your other commitments.

5. Don’t forget to count the pennies:  It’s essential that from the start, you know how your business is doing. Understanding the finances of your business is a must!  Make sure you set up an additional bank account, and keep your personal and business money separate, as you don’t want your finances getting too messy! The HMRC tend to frown at you!

6. Don’t forget to set out how you want to be paid from the start: It is up to you how you charge for services, but just make sure you know how to do this professionally and in a way that suits your business.

7. Don’t be afraid to make a change: Most start-ups are not an overnight success and you’ll have you be innovative with what it is you’re doing. Don’t be afraid to go back to the drawing board, of you think you need to tweak something, or if you come up with a better way of doing/selling your product or services. The hardest thing when starting up is to know when to let go of something!
