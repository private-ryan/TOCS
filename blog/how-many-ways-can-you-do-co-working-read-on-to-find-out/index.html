How many ways can you do co-working? Read on to find out!

By rebecca / 09 Jan 2013 / No Comments

Remote Co-working

If you live far away from the people who you’d like to collaborate with – why not try a bit of a long-distance get-together?  Get your pals on Skype,  FaceTime    or Google+ Hangouts. You can all part-take in the joys of co-working whilst working remotely in your home or studio office! Remote Co-working is already part of working culture, meaning less traveling for consultants and companies who work on differing times zones.

Casual Co-working

Co-working doesn’t have to happen in a hub or office city centre location. Jelly is a great way to find other people in your area interested in co-working and exploring the idea of collaboration. You don’t even have to have a regular ‘gathering’ place to work alongside others. Jellies can take place pretty much anywhere you want! Cafes, libraries, restaurants, the park? Even your own home! Why not put an ad or press release into your local paper and seek out other co-working enthusiasts? You never know where your new found Jelly club may end up!

Rural Co-working

If you’re a home worker and feel scattered and remote in your rural location, fear not – you don’t have to compromise on your countryside abode! There are many ways to create a collaborative community. Twitter, Facebook and other social networking sites can act as a pathway to your co-working needs and help to organise meet-ups and events. Finding potential co-workers who are not on social networks can be tricky, but you can always advertise at your nearest convenience store, or supermarket to reach those who may be interested in joining in your collaborative revolution. With the ever-increasing number of homeworkers and new technologies, companies can embrace this shift of flexi-working, from the conventional ball and chain office, meaning rural co-working is becoming more accessible.

Call of Duty Co-working 

Finding you’re procrastinating? Postponing those jobs that stretch the grey matter that bit too far?  Why not put a call out to the twitterverse? Find out if there is anyone out there near your location that can swap their skill set for yours. Give yourself a push in the right direction by getting yourself a partner to set you
a goal for your work load with their expertise, or outsource those never-ending accounts you just can’t look at alone. Agree a set time frame for working and what you want to achieve out of your collaborative mentoring.  Be sure to check in with each other to review what you’ve accomplished – to talk about what you’re going to do during the next co-working session.

Corporate Co-working

It’s not just freelancers and entrepreneurs who are jumping on the collaborative bandwagon; larger companies are turning to shared space and innovation sessions to bring out ideas from employees, creating an internal co-working movement within the corporation. Flexi-working is also helping to mainstream the idea of co-working, as employers realise that the traditional office job can be hard on the working family. Allowing workers to lessen their daily commute and increase productivity is attractive to larger organisations – and dedicated flexible space is more cost effective for firms to rent, than buy space in.

Creative Co-working

Artist groups are becoming more popular as people turn their hobbies into a more fulfilling venture alongside their 9-5 job. Groups are meeting up to support and mentor one another and actively turn empty retail spaces in shopping centres or empty buildings into co-working studios or collective gallery spaces. Councils and local authorities are keen to embrace this new trend – to boost tourism to the dwindling high street, opening up the options available to aspiring Artists and creative movements.

Co-working has diversified a lot since it’s new beginnings, and is spreading through various different movements and working cultures throughout the world. “How do you prefer to work?” This may be the most important and prevalent question open to employees of the future!
