TOCS Twinterviews Femme Fatale deadlyisthefemale.com

By rebecca / 21 Dec 2012 / No Comments

So we’ve been dying to ask Claudia Kapp about her Frome business Deadly is the Female – a gorgeous boutique for vintage fashion lovers everywhere! This morning we interviewed @DeadlyFemale live on Twitter also known as a ‘Twinterview’. It was great to see the lady behind the name and get to know the ins and outs of running a small retail business.

Why not check out how the interview panned out!

@DeadlyFemale Are you ready?

@OldChurchSchool Crumbs! I mean, yes!

What attracted you to Frome as the location for the business?

@OldChurchSchool I loved Catherine Hill & all the different independent shops – it is a rare treat to find a place like this these days.

How did you get the business started?

@OldChurchSchool Honestly? Naivety, determination & a love of 1950s glamour. Plus enormous support from my family, especially my fab mum!

You studied at the #LondonCollegeofFashion, what was the first article of clothing you ever designed?

@OldChurchSchool At LCF it was a collection of pale pink & black latex lingerie inspired by Agent Provocateur! Cringe! @OldChurchSchool But I have been ‘designing’ ever since I can remember – I think I even entered a Blue Peter competition aged about 7!

Why did you choose burlesque wear/vintage inspired clothing as your choice of product?

@OldChurchSchool The collections we stock are designed for real women & were hard to find in the UK so I felt there was a gap in the market.

What does #fashion mean to you?

@OldChurchSchool To me #fashion is a way to express myself & feel good. I don’t believe in fast fashion, I’d rather invest in some classics.

What do you attribute to the success of the brand in terms of your facebook following?

@OldChurchSchool I try to be myself as much as possible & not just focus on selling products. I love what we do & I hope it reflects that.

Do you have a long-term marketing strategy?

@OldChurchSchool I know I really should have a firm plan but I mostly try to keep evolving & taking on board any feedback we get.

Was e-commerce part of your business plan when you set up shop?

@OldChurchSchool I always knew I wanted a website too, its the best of both worlds. These days some kind of web presence is essential.

What is your previous e-commerce experience?

@OldChurchSchool Not much – I ran the Myspace page for the brand I used to work for! It was useful at the time but this makes me feel old!

What has surprised you most about your fan following?

@OldChurchSchool The support we’ve had online & in person has been amazing! We are so lucky to have such loyal (& stylish!) fans.

What is the greatest challenge you face running your business?

@OldChurchSchool Organization, finding enough hours in the day & taking things too personally. I hope to work on all these points in 2013!

Would you like to collaborate with anyone else in Frome, if so – what type of venture?

@OldChurchSchool I’d love the PoundStretcher building for a shop, prohibition style bar & retro beautician with @Lolita_Noir under 1 roof!

In another life what would you have been…

@OldChurchSchool A crazy dog lady. Oh wait, I’m already more than half way there!

And all important…With whom would you most like to have dinner?

@OldChurchSchool @ChrisIsaak - He is just dreamy & I could listen to him talk for hours. A girl can dream! #swoon pic.twitter.com/Vn8jFP1Q

Thank you so much for your time and for being such a great sport…we’ve really enjoyed Twinterviewing you!

@OldChurchSchool Oh no – thank you for asking – it was fun! Right, I’m off to pack last min orders while listening to Chris Isaak!
