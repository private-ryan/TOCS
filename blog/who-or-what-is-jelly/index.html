Who or What is Jelly?

25 Jun 2012 / No Comments

Jelly is an informal event for “collective working”. Essentially it’s home workers, small business owners and freelancers meeting together for a day to work alongside each other; with the venue, Wi-Fi and refreshments all for free. Jelly greatly differs from networking, in that the aim is not to find new clients or to sell yourself, but to just meet other people whilst you work. Although Jelly originated in the U.S. it’s really taken off in the UK, where a national movement has emerged-with over 60 organisers who put on monthly Jellies.  Big Jelly exploded on to the scene in March (2011) with a great line up of guest speakers and information for small/micro businesses, really drawing attention to the UK movement. Here at The Old Church School we see many writers, entrepreneurs, freelancers, developers and photographers attend Frome Jelly.To find out more go to Jelly for home workers

Who’s it for? No matter what you do, anyone is welcome at Jelly. Bring your laptop, phone or whatever else you need and share your talent, get to know others in a relaxed atmosphere with fun, natter and chit-chatter.
