Plan it Perfect – Andrew Denham gives us his top tips on how to organise that perfect event!

By rebecca / 26 Jul 2012 / No Comments

Andrew Denham, mastermind behind the annual Cobble Wobble and Director of The Bicycle Academy gives us his best tips for making your event a success!

1. Set your goals

Every event is different, and so it’s really important to clearly define what your goals are before starting to plan an event. In some cases events are held entirely for the benefit of the attendees, where as in others the press coverage is the main focus. Once you know what you’re trying to achieve and why, making it happen will be far easier.

 

2. Plan every detail

It’s very important to plan every detail of an event, from refining the format to assigning the job cleaning up. Make sure that you write out a full event plan, then show it to some trusted friends / colleagues for critical feedback. It’s amazing just how many different things need to be considered and no matter how small they will all add up, so make a note of all of them.

 

3. Promotion Promotion Promotion

No matter how well planned, an event with no attendees will be a failure, so it’s important to actively promote yours. Target your promotion to the people you want to attend or know about it, and use social media tools to engage with them in the build up. Creating a facebook event page can be a great way of keeping attendees informed without the need for sending emails.

 

4. Pick the right team

Working with a dependable team of people is critical!  So make sure that you consider people’s strengths and weaknesses before recruiting them into your event team. There are a wide range of skills needed for most events, so choose people who will add to the skills pool and remember that friends and family aren’t always the answer.

 

5. Tell people what happened

After the event is finished make sure that you follow up by promoting what happened (to attendees, press and your mailing list), it’s amazing how many people forget to do this, and yet the potential benefits are huge.

 

6. Get some feedback

After the dust has settled make sure that you ask your team and a selection of attendees for feedback. No matter how well planned there will always be things to refine, and it’s better to ask people for their opinions than find out through a drop in attendance, or negative murmuring.
