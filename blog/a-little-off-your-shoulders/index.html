A little off your shoulders…

By rebecca / 25 Jun 2012 / No Comments

Due to the modern lifestyle, work and family responsibilities, most of us are now engaged in a constant state of stress. So when a new or additional “stressor” occurs, we are already half way to the full “fight or flight” response and our body accelerates to an unhealthy level of stress. Because of this, we can be immediately stressed out by day to day occurrences or by little things. We can’t always stop what is causing the stress but we can learn to work with our body to manage it’s effects! Caroline Sainsot’s  workshop “Stress -what it does to us and how to work with it” held here at TOCS, covered how we need to re-establish how we react to stress, and learn to achieve a state of relaxation to enable us to have the energy to cope with the unhealthy stress levels we can reach.  This can be achieved through many ways; acupuncture, meditation, breathing exercises, diet, tapping or EFT (by tapping specific acupressure points with your fingers, this method aims to  balance the body’s energy system and can be used to treat physiological or psychological problems). Exercise can also help the body to relax and reduce hormones that take you into the readily available stress response most of us find ourselves in. Learning to deal with stress means that we can embrace life and cope better with the challenges of day to day living, instead of finding ourselves in a state of unhealthy stress at almost any given situation. This workshop was a surprisingly emotional experience for the attendees, not only did we learn and explore how we respond to stress, Caroline explained that how we react to stressful encounters now, were formed many years ago through past events or childhood experiences and we can become trapped in a cycle of a negative response.  Caroline is very passionate about helping people through their journey to combat stress and it’s effects on the body, which can manifest in many ways and cause an array of health problems. Caroline is able to draw from her own experiences of stress and is able to empathise with stressors in your life, with real and achievable methods to reduce unhealthy reactions to stressors.  We’re keen to get Caroline to do another workshop, as people found this a really beneficial experience. So watch this space for another great workshop.
