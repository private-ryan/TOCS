Behind The Tool Shed

By rebecca / 10 Aug 2012 / 1 Comment
The Toolshed Gallery is an artist run contemporary arts project space in Frome, Somerset. Established in 2012 to provide a platform for local and national artists to exhibit in a different context. We invite artists to exhibit work in both a complete and transitional stage to create a forum for discussion and experiment. We interview the Artists who set up the space.
Who is behind The Tool Shed Gallery?
So far it’s Tom Bayliss, and Tristan Stevens, but this will grow. 

What inspired you to create the space? Because we wanted to have fun and create a space that was simple and allow us to curate shows without sidelines or protocol.

Have you always had entrepreneurial ambitions to start up your own space?
Tom: I don’t think I have and I don’t think we need entrepreneurial ambitions. This isn’t a business and right now is funded by us with no interest in monetary return.
We want it to grow and expand with support, but our ambitions aren’t entrepreneurial. It lies in something else.
Tristan: I enjoy working with other artists and creating shows that we both wish to put on. I am not interested in growing as a business. I think that’s why we like to name The Tool Shed as a project space rather than a gallery. It’s about getting things out there for visual pleasure and discourse of ideas.
Describe The Tool Shed in three words…
Small, Big, Dynamic. 
How did you get the exhibition space off the ground?
Blood, Sweat, Tears, fags, coffee, beer, (overdrafts).
Who will be exhibiting at The Tool Shed?
So far we have had an offsite group show of six artists under the theme of Dystopia and a solo exhibition of David Gates at The Tool Shed. Currently it is Stephen Haines, a multi skilled artist exhibiting a life size sculpture of Iggy. Offsite on the Kelseys shop front in Frome, we have put up two large posters produced by Stephen.  We see The Tool Shed beyond the gallery walls and hope to be doing more interventions, screenings, gigs and so on. Anything, really. Coming up in September is a painter called Doris Day.
Will you be showcasing work for local Artists’ ?
We are not being specific and will invite artists wherever they are from. During the Frome festival, in contrast to David Gates (an Essex based artist) we had The Dystopia exhibition which showcased only local artists. As much as we support local art , showing national and international artists is giving Frome a chance to see something else.
What will The Tool Shed bring to the small market town of Frome? Frome is not just a small market town. It already has a wealth of creative people and opportunity. There is so much Frome already has to offer but there’s nothing wrong with having more. 
The Tool Shed is located in the Silk Mill Quarter of the town, is this an area of regeneration?
I think it is. Naming it a quarter is to express that there are already many people working here. There’s the Silk Mill Gallery, and then there is the Silk Mill Studios which are being renovated into purpose built workshops and studios. Oh, and now us! 
Who would be your ultimate exhibitor at The Tool Shed?
Tony Hart. 
Do you think Frome is an exciting place for up and coming Artists?
Considering it already has a great breath of creatives, I think it is exciting for anyone. 
Would you like to collaborate with other venue’s in the town?
There is no reason not to. We think Frome can grow as a united front, and collaborating is already what The Tool Shed is about. 
What is your favourite quote?
“You say it best when you say nothing at all.”  Ronan Keating.
