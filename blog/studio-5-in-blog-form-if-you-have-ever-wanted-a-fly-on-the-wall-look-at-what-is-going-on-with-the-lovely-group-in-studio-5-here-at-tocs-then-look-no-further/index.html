STUDIO 5, IN BLOG FORM…If you have ever wanted a fly-on-the-wall look at what is going on with the lovely group in Studio 5 here at TOCS, then look no further!

By rebecca / 23 Jul 2012 / No Comments

Sam Smith, TOCS work experience Graduate is that fly (Disclaimer: He is not a fly).

Here is his account of a typical day in the life of the famous (studio) 5!

“I’ve been buzzing around the studio for a couple of weeks, annoying everyone and bumping in to windows while the team gets on with their work and swats at me with rolled up newspapers (Disclaimer: Still not a fly).

The most used word in Studio 5 recently has been “pigeon”, with almost everyone getting involved in the project for the Royal Pigeon Racing Association. To help new fanciers get in to the sport, the Ghost team have completely redesigned the RPRA website, Doubleshot have made a film at The British Homing World Show of the Year, Avalanche Media have been promoting the sport and countless odd facts about pigeons have been discovered. Did you know pigeons can’t poo while flying? Amaze your friends with this knowledge!

 

The Doubleshot team have had a very busy couple of months. They have been working with Generation Next to look in to the future of culture, science and business. As if observing premonitions wasn’t enough, Jade’s first fiction film ‘Common Law’ was entered in to the Cannes Film Festival. When there was a spare moment, Doubleshot recently made new websites for local businesses Annette Gabbedey Jewellery and Pullin Rose, as well as one for Olivia Rubin’s new collection. Gosh!

 

Tim has been out and about, shooting photos for the University of Bath prospectus and the Olympic torch relay. He also recently got an iPhone, a big upgrade from his last phone. In between playing games, he has been incorporating how well the camera on it works in to his business photography training, Photo Espresso. I’m not sure if that makes his iPhone a business expense or not.

Cole has been improving his inter-office relations by working with Ed from Doubleshot and Gavin, the captain of The Old Church School, on a brand new website for Nature Paint, an eco-friendly paint company. He has promised that I will be able to get involved in some of the development too, using very appealing words like “data management”. Also, some breaking news. Cole has just updated his Tiny V website to teach us all a little more about the footwear of cobbler’s children.

Avalanche Media are currently knee deep in pigeons. Not only are they promoting the Royal Pigeon Racing Association, they are helping to make ways for fanciers to protect their pigeons from predators. When Avalanche aren’t saving pigeons, they’re trying to save pubs. One ofBath’s oldest pubs, the Packhorse Inn, was closed in May, but Avalanche Media are getting involved with campaigning to ‘Save the Packhorse’. They are also working with the Archangel in Frome to promote the ‘One for Ruby’ fund raising events and the new cocktail menu, because you can never have too many friends that own pubs.

Ben has been working with the Pencil Design Agency downstairs on a new website for a design tool made by HP. I’ve tried peeking over his shoulder to get a look at it, but all the code on the screen looks like something out of an early 2000’s hacker movie. Complicated stuff, but Ben knows what he’s doing. He has also created a new website for Motivation, a weight management clinic in Ireland, and did it without having to stay up all night working, which he is quite happy about.

The Ghost team have finished building the Royal Pigeon Racing Association’s website, so they’re back to work on other projects. They have made a new website for The Prince’s Accounting for Sustainability Project and an area resource tool for St. Mungo’s homeless charity. Also, fun fact! David and Nicola both have a history working with books, which is why they thought it would be fun to get back in to that and make a website for the Marlborough Literary Festival.

As you can see, everybody is pretty busy, so it’s surprising how happy they all seem. There’s always a smile or a joke going round or a video of musical cats up in Studio 5. Either they really do enjoy their work, or they’re very good at hiding their emotions…

(We’re pretty sure it’s the former!)
