How to Not Lose Friends & Alienate People-10 tips for pain free collaboration

21 Jun 2012 / No Comments

Finding a creative place to work for a dynamic small business can be a challenge for anyone at times, however co-working spaces such as The Old Church School can make this easier. Co-working often leads to collaboration and we see this happen every day! But what are the things to consider when collaborating with co-workers in a work hub?

1.Go with the flow Let the socialisation happen at its own pace. Co-working spaces are a relaxed and informal place to work; the hard sell act doesn’t go down very well in these places, so just let the coffee flow and get to know people naturally. Ideas and talk will start flowing when it’s the right time!

2.Don’t be a wall flower Getting to genuinely know your fellow co-workers will make it easier for you to build relationships that can lead to collaboration. A quick chat here and a quick giggle there can progress to work related queries and conundrums-where you might just have the answer to a seemingly unanswerable question.

3.Play nice In a co-working space, you are going to hear other people’s opinions and projects. Be respectful and show that you are a trustworthy sort of fellow-anything you over hear or think may be of a sensitive nature keep it to yourself (check out our terms and conditions). This will facilitate and assist collaboration. Also keep in mind that you’re going to be open to others’ ideas and suggestions in an open plan office, so don’t alienate yourself by throwing your weight around-you’ll end up at the back of the class with no friends-sob, sob!

4.Honesty is the best policy If someone wants to pick your brain about something, be genuine and honest (obviously without hurting anyone’s feelings!) about what it is they’re doing. After all they’ve come to you for advice out of respect for what it is you can offer to their project.

5.Share the love If someone approaches you with an idea or problem and you feel out of your depth, suggest someone who can help or knows more about what it is they’re after. Don’t say you can do something because you want the glory or want that collaborative moment-your time will come. Share and share alike, is a great motto for co-working!

6.Express yourself  You’ve decided to collaborate on a project-set up a communication system that suits your working style. Whether this is a chat in a meeting room or an informal get-together at your desk in the open plan area, make sure you have open channels of communication.

7.Agree to disagree Disagreements are going to come up if you’re working with someone else, or in a group. Though your venture may have an informal feel to it, stick a few formalities’ in there-that way if something does go awry, you protect all interested parties and still stay chums at the coffee machine at the end of the project!

8.Keep your hands clean Who does what and why? Make sure you know what you and others bring to your collaborative project, and stick to it. Don’t suddenly go off course without letting others know you’ve had a light bulb moment!

9.Trust is a must Collaboration is based on “The common good”. People work best together on the grounds you are all in it for the same reason/s and are being true to each other. If someone is not a bona fide team player, they’ll show their true colours soon enough. Unfortunately the spirit of collaboration can easily go of course, so make sure you know who you’re working with before you embark on that venture!

10.Two heads are better than one If you find out you’re pitching for the same piece of work as a fellow co-worker, why don’t you collaborate? Can you do a better job together than apart? Is collaboration better than competition? We’ll let you decide!

We hope these hints and tips are of use, if you’ve collaborated with a fellow co-worker, why not get in touch and tell us firsthand what it’s like and pass on your pearls of wisdom… headgirl@theoldchuchschool.co.uk
