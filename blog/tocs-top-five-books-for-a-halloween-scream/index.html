TOCS Top Five Books for a Halloween Scream

05 Oct 2012 / No Comments

So, as Halloween approaches and you’re not loving all the hype and cheesy films on t.v – why not escape with a good book. We’ve jotted down our top five creepy reads for a cosy night in by the fire.

1. Carrie: Of course Stephen King has to be top of the list! This is a great book for an evening of reading, as it’s a short story.  A good choice if you find a book difficult to get in to, as the story is structured around interviews, newspaper and magazine articles – exposing the story pretty quickly. It’s fast paced and has a strong plot that keeps you wanting more.  What’s it about? A shy high-school girl, who is a misfit in every sense of the word, from her home-to high school….Except Carrie has telekinetic powers! Sick of all the teasing she goes through at school, Carrie uses her powers to help stand up for herself…with dramatic consequences.

2. Shutter Island: By Dennis Lehane. This is a great book, another short one for All Hallows’ Eve, as the book throws you into an elaborate plot, with an amazing twist pretty much round every corner! What’s it about? U.S. Marshal Edward ‘Teddy’ Daniels, is investigating a psychiatric facility on a tiny  island, in pursuit of an escaped patient – or so it seems. We won’t give any more away, as it is a thrilling read!

3. The Night Stalker: By Chris Carter (no, not the X-Files guy!). This is another short book, very fast paced, and is hard to put down! Descriptive – yet doesn’t waste your time with poetic waffling. The book is one of four in a series based around ‘ Detective Robert Hunter’. An enjoyable, yet typical macho main character in law enforcement.  What’s it about? When an unidentified female body is discovered perfectly laid to rest on a slab in an grotty old butcher’s shop, the cause of death is unclear. The body bares no marks; except for the fact that her lips have been carefully stitched shut (if you have a weak disposition – it gets worse, so this may not be for you!), the book is an unraveling tale of terror, with a ticking clock – all involved in the case must work against the clock to save lives.

4. Wide Acre: By Philippa Gregory. This is a shocking book, but is a fantastic read. It is a large book and one of a trilogy – but perfect for cosy night in by yourself! The Author has a lovely way in which she writes and it’s easy to get in to. What’s it about? Don’t be fooled by the title ‘Lady’. Beatrice Lacey, as willful as she is beautiful and doesn’t conform to the social customs of her time (the book is set in the eighteenth century). Destined to lose her identity and childhood home once she’s palmed off to be wed, Beatrice uses all means necessary to stay on the land she loves. Seduction, betrayal, even murder!  Beatrice’s mission is without apology or remorse. You’ll love to hate her!

5. The Haunted: By Niki Valentine. Again another short book, not as fast paced, but worth the wait for a thrilling fight for survival. What’s it about? Set in the Scottish highlands, main charterers Martin and Sue decide to escape their usual pace of life and head out for a weekend of ‘back-to-basics’ living in an abandoned shack, that Martin knew as a child. Bickering leads to violent arguments, and more. With Sue getting more paranoid and Martin losing his patience with his wife. Their lives will never be the same again!
