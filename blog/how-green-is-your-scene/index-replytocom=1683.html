How green is your scene?

25 Jun 2012 / 2 Comments

Do you want to green up your business? You maybe greener than you think! Ask yourself these questions to see how eco-friendly you really are!

1.Does your marketing leave a paper trail? Do you minimise material waste by maintaining an active blog or social media platforms? You can market your brand free and green, with very little waste impact. We send out a monthly enewsletter and love to have a goss on Twitter and Facebook

2.Do you recycle? Here at TOCS we are big on recycling-with two recycling centres in the hub. We sort out waste; to ensure we are left with very little for landfill.We recycle everything we can: Office stuff like toner cartridges, batteries, cardboard and glass-aswell as the usual suspects, like paper, metal and plastics. Veolia is a good company that collect and manage recyclable waste.

3.Do you like to save trees? You can, by using recycled or eco certified paper in your office! Certified paper, such as FSC paper, means that the paper comes from forests managed responsibly. Here are a couple of online suppliers who can help – alocalprinter.com  and theegreenoffice Here at TOCS we try to use as much environmentally friendly paper as possible and any waste paper is made into a scrap book for jotting down notes – which is then recycled when it’s been used!

4.How do you advertise? If you have any paper marketing material-have you sourced a printer who offers certified or recycled paper? Do you advertise in local papers or alternative magazines? Locally The Spark or more nationally Recognise are aimed at those who have a green conscious. Advertising in places such as health food shops, local cafes and web sites devoted to eco-friendly topics will show you’re keen to be green!

5.Do you give out freebies? Are they as planet friendly as they could be?  Stuff like pencils, shopping bags or t-shirts (made from sustainable/recycled materials) are a great alternative to disposable knick-knacks. Pencil give away giant pencils as prizes and also have many giveaways that are eco friendly! They’re a good bunch to ask about sustainable merchandise for your company.

6. Do you get your green seen? Could you use your environmental efforts as a selling point by featuring it in your marketing? We’ve signed up to 10:10, which we’ve sneakily mentioned in our email signatures, so people know we’re working towards sustainability. Why not sign up too and see what you can do…Let people know that you offer these green services and they’ll probably spread the word to their friends!

7.Do you meet and greet? Local networking can develop your knowledge on all things green and help others to be more environmentally friendly! Have you checked out other businesses, sustainable or eco-friendly groups in your area? We have Sustainable Frome and Frome Car Club which is sponsored by The Old Church School.

8.Do you get the most from your host? Is your web host as environmentally friendly as it could be? You could be helping to save on emissions by using a web host that strives to ensure your website makes very little environmental impact. Here are a couple of examples of companies that think about the environment, but there are many more out there!  Supergreenhosting.co.uk  and ukfast.co.uk

9.Do you practice what you preach? Do you offer green services to clients or customers where you can? Do you have fun with your recycling or eco ways? TOCS now has two composters and we’re even growing chillies in some of our home made compost!  We’ve also fitted filters on our taps to reduce our water waste.

10.Do you clean green? Do you use products that are tough on grime and nice at the same time? Here at TOCS, we use environmentally friendly cleaning products where we can, from hand soap and washing up liquid to air fresheners that are planet and animal friendly!

We’re keen to be green…we hope you are too!
