Why does my business need a blog?

By rebecca / 25 Jun 2012 / No Comments

That’s a pretty good question! Our Guest Blogger  Andy Britnell, AKA See a man about a blog  has given us 5 good reasons for having a blog for your small business! Read on for Andy’s pearls of wisdom…

One of the fundamentals of internet marketing is to own as much of the internet as possible. You want as many people as possible to encounter your business, your products and have the ability to trade with you online in as many places as possible. That means having multiple presences on the web. So why have a blog? There are five main reasons why a blog is a good strategy for increasing your businesses visibility and credibility online:

1. Integration with social media (SM) – allows you to link up your blog and social media accounts. You link with your customers via Twitter, Facebook, LinkedIn etc and they can be signposted to your blog. They can also link with your SM accounts via your blog.

2. Search Engine Optimisation (SEO) – Google loves blogs when they are updated on a regular basis and the database structure is easily mined by the search robots.

3. Targeted traffic – niche your blog so that you get visitors who are looking for your specific product or service. The same goes for social media where you can direct prospective customers to your business.

4. Building a community of raving fans – you don’t need 10’s of thousands of random visitors – just 1000 or so people who really love what you do and are willing to shout about it!

5. Ability to monetise your business online – a blog is another channel to market where you can sell your wares, sell related items from affiliate sources or sell information products which help your customers solve a problem, save time or save money.

Setting up a blog is relatively easy, although it is useful to be clear about what market you are in, what niche you are going to target and whether or not the competition has more muscle and resources than you. Choose the wrong battle and you’re toast, pick the right one and your business could boom.

Andy Britnell helps businesses build visibility online with a down to earth approach and straightforward language.
