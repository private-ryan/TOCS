A great introduction to bookkeeping!

21 Jun 2012 / No Comments

We had a fantastic workshop with Barbara Bacon on the 24 May. Barbara who herself has run her own home-based bookkeeping business for 17 years, was able to take the mystic out of bookkeeping for our attendees. The workshop broke down the details of businesses structures to consider when starting up, with helpful advice, insight and websites that could help the small business or start-up, in a minefield of information – from choosing a company name, website domains, to banking advice, especially for the cash business. Barbara delivered the work shop with eloquence and a gentle nature that put everyone at ease, really understanding the side of the small business owner. Bamboozling accountancy terms were explained in detail, so the small businesses could really leave with knowing how to approach professionals within the bookkeeping and accounting world with their needs, or even go it alone – with great tips on how to do in-house record keeping and understand how to layout a spreadsheet in keeping with bookkeeping professionals and accountants. Costing, expenditure and pre-trading expenses for the small business were a key element in the workshop, so that the start-up could identify the legal requirements of setting up – with failsafe tips to ensure that the business was started right and on the path to success! Employment was also covered with useful information on the minimum rates of pay, National Insurance and payroll legalities. The workshop was finished off with tips for finding the right bookkeeper for you (if you needed one), along with tips on how to find an accountant. The workshop was a really useful introduction into the world of starting up and bookkeeping. We’re so pleased so many people came and benefited from Barbara’s wisdom and we’ll be running another workshop on 28 June, so if you missed this one – be sure to book onto the next <a href=”http://www.eventbrite.co.uk/event/3620842036″>here</a>!
